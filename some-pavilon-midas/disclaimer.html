1. Nature of Forex Markets

The foreign exchange (forex) market is one of the largest and most liquid financial markets in the world, operating 24 hours a day, five days a week. However, the same features that make forex attractive — leverage, volatility, and 24-hour accessibility — also make it inherently risky.

Forex prices can be highly volatile and can move rapidly in unfavorable directions due to a wide range of factors including economic data releases, geopolitical events, central bank policy decisions, market sentiment, and unexpected global events. Such movements can result in significant financial losses within very short time frames.

3. Forex Trading Pool — Investment Risk Disclosure

Our Forex Trading Pool allows members to contribute capital to a collectively managed forex trading fund. Participation in the pool carries the following specific risks which you must fully understand before investing:

3.1 Capital Risk
You may lose some or all of your invested capital. The projected monthly returns of 10%–15% are targets based on our trading strategy performance and current market conditions. They are NOT guaranteed returns. There is no guarantee that our traders will achieve these targets in any given month.

3.2 Market Risk
Adverse market conditions, including but not limited to sudden currency crises, geopolitical shocks, liquidity crises, and extreme volatility events, can result in significant drawdowns or losses of the pooled capital.

3.3 Management Risk
While our trading team employs professional risk management practices, human error, system failures, or unforeseen circumstances may result in trading losses beyond expected parameters.

3.4 Liquidity Risk
Pool investments are locked for a minimum of one monthly cycle. Early withdrawal is not permitted within the cycle period. In extraordinary market conditions, we reserve the right to delay withdrawal processing with prior notice to investors.

3.5 Regulatory Risk
Changes in regulations governing forex trading or collective investment schemes in Ghana, Nigeria, or globally may affect our ability to continue operations or return capital.

3.6 Counterparty Risk
We trade through third-party brokers and platforms. Failure of these counterparties, including broker insolvency, could affect pooled funds.
