ZERO TOLERANCE POLICY

Pavilion Midas Academy Ltd has a zero-tolerance stance toward money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crimes. We cooperate fully with relevant regulatory and law enforcement authorities to prevent, detect, and report any activity that may constitute financial crime through our forex signal subscription services, Forex Trading Pool, and referral commission program.

Effective Date: January 2025  |  Issued by: Pavilion Midas Academy Ltd

1. Purpose and Commitment

Pavilion Midas Academy Ltd is committed to the highest standards of Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance. This policy establishes our framework to prevent, detect, and report any activity that may constitute money laundering, terrorist financing, or other financial crimes through our forex signal subscription services, Forex Trading Pool, and referral commission program.

We have a zero-tolerance stance toward money laundering and will cooperate fully with relevant regulatory and law enforcement authorities.

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All registered clients and investors on our platform
  • All referral affiliates receiving commission payments
  • All employees, management, and third-party partners of Pavilion Midas Academy Ltd
  • All financial transactions including subscription payments, pool investment deposits, and referral commission payouts

3. Definition of Money Laundering

Money laundering is the process of concealing the origins of illegally obtained funds by passing them through a sequence of banking transfers, commercial transactions, or other means to make them appear legitimately earned. For our purposes, this includes:

  • Placement — introducing illegal funds into our platform
  • Layering — disguising the source through multiple transactions
  • Integration — withdrawing cleaned funds as legitimate earnings

4. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

4.1 Know Your Customer (KYC)
All clients must complete identity verification before accessing our services. Required documentation includes:

  • Full legal name and date of birth
  • Government-issued photo identification (national ID, passport, or driver's license)
  • Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement not older than 3 months)
  • Valid email address and phone number
  • Source of funds declaration for pool investments above $1,000

4.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
Enhanced verification is required for:

  • Pool investment deposits exceeding $5,000 in a single transaction
  • Clients identified as Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
  • Clients from high-risk jurisdictions as identified by the FATF
  • Any transaction that appears unusual or inconsistent with the client's profile

4.3 Ongoing Monitoring
Client accounts are subject to continuous monitoring. We reserve the right to request updated documentation at any time, particularly when transaction behavior changes significantly from established patterns.

5. Prohibited Clients and Transactions

Pavilion Midas Ltd will not onboard or transact with:

  • Individuals or entities on international sanctions lists (OFAC, UN, EU)
  • Clients unable or unwilling to provide identity verification
  • Anonymous or pseudonymous accounts (excluding cryptocurrency wallet addresses used for payment only)
  • Individuals known to be engaged in criminal activity
  • Shell companies without identifiable beneficial owners
  • Clients from FATF blacklisted jurisdictions

We reserve the right to reject any payment or refund any deposit that we reasonably suspect originates from illegal activity.

6. Transaction Monitoring

6.1 Red Flag Indicators
Our team monitors for the following suspicious patterns:

  • Unusually large deposits inconsistent with a client's stated financial profile
  • Multiple small deposits structured to avoid reporting thresholds (structuring)
  • Rapid withdrawal of funds immediately after deposit with no trading or investment activity
  • Use of multiple payment methods or accounts for a single client
  • Third-party payments — funds received from a name different to the registered account holder
  • Requests to redirect commission payouts to accounts not matching the registered affiliate
  • Pressure to process transactions quickly without explanation
  • Clients who are evasive about the source of their funds

6.2 Transaction Limits

  • Minimum pool investment: $100
  • Transactions above $5,000 require enhanced documentation
  • Unusual transaction patterns trigger automatic internal review

7. Referral Commission Payments — AML Controls

All referral affiliates receiving commission payouts must:

  • Be registered and verified members of our platform
  • Have completed full KYC verification
  • Receive payments only to accounts or wallets registered in their own name
  • Provide source of referral activity upon request

We do not pay commissions to unverified third parties, anonymous wallets unlinked to a verified identity, or affiliates who cannot demonstrate legitimate referral activity.

8. Cryptocurrency Payment Controls

Where clients pay via cryptocurrency (processed through CoinPayments):

  • Wallet addresses must be linked to the verified account holder
  • We do not accept payments from mixers, tumblers, or privacy coins where the source cannot be traced
  • Incoming crypto transactions are screened against blockchain analytics where available
  • Refunds are returned only to the originating wallet address

9. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

9.1 Internal Reporting
Any employee or team member who identifies suspicious activity must report it immediately to the designated AML Compliance Officer. Reports must include transaction details, client information, and the nature of the suspicion.

9.2 External Reporting
Where internal review confirms reasonable grounds for suspicion, Pavilion Midas Ltd will file a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) with the relevant financial intelligence authority in our operating jurisdiction.

9.3 No Tipping Off
Under no circumstances will any employee or officer inform a client or affiliate that they are the subject of a suspicious activity report or investigation. Tipping off is a serious breach of this policy and applicable law.

10. Record Keeping

Pavilion Midas Ltd will retain the following records for a minimum of 5 years:

  • All KYC and identity verification documents
  • Transaction records including deposits, withdrawals, and commission payments
  • Suspicious activity reports and supporting documentation
  • Client account history and communication logs

Records will be made available to relevant authorities upon lawful request.

11. Staff Training and Awareness

All personnel involved in client onboarding, payment processing, and financial operations will receive AML awareness training covering recognition of suspicious activity, KYC procedures, and reporting obligations. Training will be reviewed and updated annually.

12. AML Compliance Officer

Pavilion Midas Ltd designates an AML Compliance Officer responsible for:

  • Overseeing implementation of this policy
  • Reviewing and approving high-risk client onboarding
  • Reviewing suspicious activity reports
  • Liaising with regulatory authorities
  • Maintaining and updating this policy

Contact: compliance@pavilonmidas.com

13. Policy Review

This AML Policy will be reviewed annually or whenever there is a material change in our business operations, applicable laws, or regulatory guidance. Updates will be communicated to all relevant staff and reflected on our platform.

AML Compliance Statement

Pavilion Midas Academy Ltd is dedicated to maintaining the highest standards of financial integrity. We continuously monitor and update our AML procedures to comply with evolving regulations and best practices.
